Stanley Kubrick (July 26, 1928 - March 7, 1999) was an American film director, producer, screenwriter and photographer. Widely considered one of the greatest filmmakers of all time, his films span a number of genres and are known for their intense attention to detail, innovative cinematography, extensive set design and dark humor.
